History(1960-1990)

At its inception, digital art marked a relationship between artists and engineers/scientists, which explored the connections between art and technology.

. The First experimentations with computers by artists were made in the early 1960’s, notably by John Whitney, who created the first computer generated art using mathematical functions to transform visual components while making art. The term “digital art” was first used in 1980 after computer engineers created a paint program known as AARON, which was programmed to make large drawings on sheets of paper. With the advent of the internet during the 1990’s which made digital more accessible to artists, experimentations with artists could use the interactive elements of the internet to create art. Improvements in digital technology made it easier to share and download video onto a computer, allowing images and film to be digitally edited.

Present

Digital art revolutionized the way art could be made, distributed, viewed, and easily transported and seen via the television, computer screen, social media, or internet. This has empowered artists to create their own careers without the necessity of representation, utilizing contemporary tools like crowdsourcing to fund their work, and the potential to go viral to spread their art into the mainstream consciousness.
